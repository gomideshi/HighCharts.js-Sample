Texas Music Industry Listings, 1991-2014

A Tale Over Time

The data depicted in the graph above was sourced from the Texas Music Office, and demonstrates the shift in volume for registered industry positions in the state of Texas. This value encompasses a wide variety of employment options for Texas musicians: private individuals, performing groups, and even teachers are included.

Since the start of the annual record in 1998, the amount of listings has risen from a modest 1,160 to that of over 18,000. That's a total increase of over 1400% over 23 years.

But the question remains: what does 'typical employment' look like for the individuals within this industry?



Employment Breakdown for Texas Musicians, 2014

Counting Heads

At the time of this writing, the total volume of employment among registered musicians in the state of Texas amounts to over 135,000 people. And, as one might expect, such a large body of people translates into a massive volume of potential forms of employment.

According to the data, the largest share of individuals that belong to this category are, unsurprisingly, working as musicians. The second and third largest groups, representing 15% each respectively, belong to performing groups and touring services. Following this work we see nightclubs, radio work, and academia enter the picture.

Needless to say, the data is unambiguous: Texas Musicians are doing a whole lot more than just making music.
